Layered instruments

Revision: 2000.04.04 14:25 GMT+5


For those of you who have already seen Reaktor - its similiar to its concept of separate blocks (inputs/generators/filters/etc)...

The main idea is to create instruments from those blocks. Even sample-based instruments will be built out of such blocks.

Blocks are divided into two types - instrument and sample processing blocks. Instrument processing blocks operate on instrument-related entities such as note, pitch, volume, velocity and panning. One of such blocks may be "note-pass selector": it inputs a note issued by instrument track, checks it against internal note mapping table and routes to appropriate sample output block, which in turn plays some sample. This is emulation of usual note-sample mapping table. But output of note-pass may be for example routed to pitch-pass selector which will select an output based on pitch of the note. This architecture allows us to create extremely sophisticated layers without writing any scripts.

Advantages:
- easy creating of any layering algorithm
- created hierarchy of blocks can be compiled for in-line execution without loss for table traversing
